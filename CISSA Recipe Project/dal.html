Dal Bukhara

    Ingredients (Serves 2)

  • 1 cup black lentils
  • ¼ cup kidney beans
  • 4 cups of water
  • 3 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 large tomatoes, pureed
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • ½ cup fresh or heavy cream
  • Chopped cilantro (coriander leaves) for garnish

Recipe

1. Wash the black lentils and kidney beans thoroughly under running water. Soak them in 4 cups of water overnight or for at least 6-8 hours. This will help soften the lentils and reduce cooking time.

2. Drain and rinse the soaked lentils and kidney beans. In a large pot, add the soaked lentils, kidney beans, and 4 cups of water. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it simmer until the lentils and beans are tender. This might take about 30-40 minutes. You can also use a pressure cooker to expedite the process.

3. In a separate pan, heat 3 tablespoons of ghee or oil over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.

4. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears. Add the tomato puree and cook until the mixture thickens and the ghee starts to separate from the sides.

5.Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin powder, and garam masala. Cook for a few minutes, allowing the spices to blend well.

6. Once the lentils and beans are cooked, add them to the tomato-spice mixture.Mix well, add salt to taste, and allow the dal to simmer on low heat for about 20-30 minutes. This helps the flavors meld together.

7. Pour in the fresh cream or heavy cream and mix well. Let it cook for another 10 minutes,
stirring occasionally. In a small pan, heat 2 tablespoons of ghee for tempering. Add cumin seeds
and let them splutter. Add chopped garlic and dried red chili (if using) and sauté until
the garlic turns golden.

8. Pour the tempering over the prepared dal makhani.

9. Garnish with chopped cilantro (coriander leaves). Serve hot with steamed rice, naan, or roti.
